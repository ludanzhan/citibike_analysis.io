Citibike Analysis

Background

Citi Bike is a public bicycle sharing system serving the New York City boroughs. A public bicycle sharing system is a shared transport service in which bicycles are made available for shared use to individuals on a short-term basis for a price or free. Citi Bike was first proposed in 2008 by the New York City Department of Transportation and was officially opened in May 2013 with 332 stations and 6,000 bikes. Through the past eight years, Citi Bike has scuccefuly expend its average ridership numbers to above 100,000.

Project Objective

This project provides a insight into the Citi Bike past user growth history and also how the user growth effected under the current pandemic situation. Data for this for this project coudl be find in the Citi Bike open data source website.
