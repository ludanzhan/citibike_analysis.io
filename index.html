Citibike Analysis

Background

As one of the most popular eco-friendly commute tools, bike is especially well-known for its effects on environmentally friendly, daily exercise, and money savings. With the emerging public bicycle sharing system, Citi Bike has expanded the usage and popularity of bike riding. Over the past eight years, Citi Bike has successfully grown average ridership numbers to above 100,000.

Project Objective

This project aims to provide a deep insight into the Citi Bike history including the user demographic distribution (age, gender, and user type), time-series analysis (overall bike usage growth and the influence of covid), and the station's geographic distribution. All data used in this project can be found in the Citi Bike public data site

Citibike Dashboard

User Demographic Information

As shown in the graph, the majority of the Citibike Users' ages are in the range of 30 to 39 years old. The second most popular age range is 40 to 49 years old. Data used for this graph is collected in 2021 with over 100,000 records.

As shown in the pie chart, over 70% of the users have paid membership plan with the Citi Bike

As shown in the graph, the overall number of biker users displays a continuously increasing trend from 2014 to 2019. Gender-wise, we see the number of male users largely outnumber the number of female users.

Time Series Analysis

This time series chart displays the daily sums of trips from the year 2014 to 2022. The overall bike usage steadily increases over the year. We also observe the impact of Covid on bike usage, the Covid started in early 2020 and was followed by a city-wide lockdown period which displays in the graph that bike usage didn't grow as expected.

This graph calculates the average number of hourly trips for every hour during the day for the whole year. We observe two pick hours during the day and the afternoon at 8 am and 5 pm. This trend makes sense because those two peak hours are the regular working and off-working schedules for the majority of people.

Station Distribution Map
